        🎧
        Audio-Based Listening Scoring
      
        
      
        
        SmartILR rates two dimensions separately for audio submissions:
        the ILR Language Level from linguistic complexity (discourse structure, inference demand, vocabulary abstraction)
        and the Listening Difficulty (Easy / Moderate / Difficult) from delivery conditions
        (speech rate, clarity, noise, accent, speakers).
        Any transcript shown below is provided only for teacher reference and is never used in scoring.
        
          ✓ Delivery conditions (speed, clarity, noise) do not raise or lower the ILR language level.
          The same audio always produces the same ratings regardless of whether a transcript is present.
        
        Skill: Listening  ·  Input: Audio  ·  Scoring: Direct Audio Model
      
        
    
        

    
    
        
      
        
        Assessment Guidance Notice
      
        
      
        
        
        
        
        
        This SmartILR rating is intended as a decision-support tool for instructors. The generated level reflects an automated analysis of discourse features, inference demands, structural complexity, and—when applicable—listening delivery conditions.
        
        
        However, ILR level assignment ultimately requires professional instructional judgment. Teachers may consider additional factors such as learner population, instructional context, curriculum alignment, and assessment purpose.
        
        
        Teachers may also use the Linguistic View section to review structural and lexical features such as parts of speech, discourse connectors, and selected word complexity when confirming or questioning the automated rating.
        
        
        If an instructor disagrees with the assigned level, they may record their justification in the Teacher Feedback field above. The final authority for level assignment remains with the teacher.
